About Me

Richard Sambasivam is a writer of stories based on crime, history, and public policy, always interested in the question of why the world is the way it is and what we can do to change it. His most recent work is the “British Global Ambitions and Indian Identity,” in the essay collection The American Revolution: A World War (Smithsonian Press 2018). On weekdays, he separates himself from his novel manuscript to analyze country risk for the Export-Import Bank of the United States.

Richard grew up in the Los Angeles area. He earned a bachelor's in economics from UCLA and a master's in public policy from Princeton University. He lives in Washington, D.C.
